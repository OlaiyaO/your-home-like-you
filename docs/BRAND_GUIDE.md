# Your Home Like You Brand Guide

## Positioning

Your Home Like You is a property transformation company. It coordinates the services needed to dress, care for, transform, buy, sell, or improve a property.

Primary promise: **One accountable team. From first idea to finished space.**

Tagline: **Property, made personal.**

## Naming

Use **Your Home Like You** without a comma in formal writing. Do not abbreviate the customer-facing name to YHLY except in internal references or highly constrained applications.

## Logo

The mark combines an open architectural frame with a Y-shaped threshold. It represents a home shaped around the person entering it, without relying on a generic closed-house symbol. Use the red mark on ivory, paper, or light photography and the white mark on red, ink, or dark photography.

Keep clear space equal to at least one quarter of the mark width. Do not stretch, rotate, outline, shadow, or recolor the mark outside the approved palette.

## Colour

- Signature red: `#C81522`
- Deep red: `#98101A`
- Architectural ink: `#181512`
- Warm ivory: `#F6F0E8`
- Paper: `#FFFDFC`
- Stone: `#D8CEC2`

Red is a signature, not wallpaper. Use ink and ivory for breathing room and reserve red for calls to action, identifiers, and decisive brand moments.

## Typography

- Display: DM Serif Display
- Body and interface: Manrope

Use sentence case. Headlines should be short, human, and outcome-led. Avoid dense uppercase text except for labels and metadata.

## Voice

Sound assured, practical, warm, and specific. Explain what happens next. Avoid unsupported superlatives such as “best,” “leading,” or “number one.” Never invent project counts, years in business, testimonials, credentials, or locations.

Preferred language:

- “Tell us what must change.”
- “We inspect and define the work.”
- “You approve a clear proposal.”
- “We deliver and hand over.”

Avoid vague language such as “turning dreams into reality” and “your trusted partner.”

## Photography

Use warm, architectural natural light; tactile materials; completed details; people actively measuring, making, inspecting, or handing over; and credible before-and-after sequences. Lifestyle campaigns should show modestly dressed Nigerian families in candid moments of belonging, shared joy, safety, pride, and continuity. People should interact with one another or admire the space rather than pose for the camera. Preserve calm negative space when photography carries editorial copy. Do not present generated or stock references as company work.

## Documents

Invoices, proposals, letterheads, site boards, uniforms, social templates, and vehicle graphics should use the mark at a consistent small scale with generous white space. Commercial documents should prioritize legibility over decoration.

### Business card

- Finished size: 90 × 50 mm.
- Add 3 mm bleed and keep names and contact details at least 5 mm from the trim edge.
- Use the ink front for the company promise and the ivory back for individual details.
- Replace all template fields with approved information before print.

### Company stamp

- Standard face: 38 mm round, one colour only.
- Use signature red for brand correspondence or black/blue ink where an administrative process requires it.
- Do not add “registered,” a registration number, or legal-entity wording until those details are verified.
- Test the final physical stamp at actual size; preserve the heavier simplified line weight.

### Letterhead

- Size: A4, 210 × 297 mm.
- Keep body copy inside a minimum 20 mm safe area.
- Use the red edge rule only on first pages; continuation pages may omit it.
- Footer legal, registration, address, tax, and contact details remain blocked until verified.

### Operational applications

- Site boards should lead with the promise and use high-contrast ink, ivory, and red.
- Workwear should use the mark only at small chest scale; do not place long copy on uniforms.
- Document folders should remain mostly ivory with one red identifier and clear project metadata.
- Social avatars use the mark alone, never the full name at unreadable sizes.
